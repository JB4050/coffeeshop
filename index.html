The Coffee process

Plant to Cup

1

Planting

Our Coffee Partners plant their seeds by hand, providing the perfect conditions to grow the very best beans. These seeds won't produce coffee 'cherries' for 3-4 years!

2

Harvest time!

After a long wait, the cherries are ready to be harvested. As the cherries ripen at different speeds, they must be hand-picked to ensure they are ripe enough.

3

The wait is on...Again!

Once the beans are extracted from the cherries, they are washed and left to age for anywhere between 6 months to 3 years! This vital step smoothens the taste profile and reduces acidity.

4

Grading and Roasting

Our expert quality-assurance teams grade the beans based on appearance, flavour, and aroma, before sending them to be roasted to one of three levels: light, medium, or dark.

5

The most important part- You!

The final step begins with a few clicks as you decide which beans suit you best. However you like your beans ground and brewed, they come to serve their ultimate purpose in your cup!

Our Story

  • Our story starts and ends with the delicious coffee bean, but our company sprouted from the magnificent red-brick warehouses of Northern England.

  • 1760s

    Coffee was fast becoming the drink-of-choice for many Europeans, and demand was skyrocketing. London alone accommodated over 3,000 coffee houses!

  • 1771

    The first purpose-built storage facilites for coffee beans brought to the United Kingdom was created in Kingston-upon-Hull. These beautiful red-bricked warehouses held up to 120 tonnes of coffee beans at once (the smell must've been intoxicating!)

  • 1923

    The fabulous red-brick warehouses were decommissioned after a stunning 152 years of service. They were due to be demolished but were saved by local businessman Herbert Gardener.

  • 1950s

    Passing into the personal portfolio of Gardener, a property tycoon, saw the warehouses given a second life as a gathering place for local couples, hosting dances and parties well into the second half of the 20th century. (It was stated as late as the 70s that certain areas of the former warehouses still smelled of coffee!)

  • 2008

    The warehouses were passed to the local council, who began renting them out as office-space. An intrepid local coffee-enthusiast took the plunge, creating a coffee distribution business with great aspirations, but that never forgot its roots!
